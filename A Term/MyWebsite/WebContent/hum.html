HUM

Humanities at Mass Academy is taught by Mr. Ludt. So far, we've written, rehearsed, and performed skits based on Henry David Thoreau's Walden.

We have also read love poems in Deir El-Medina, a slave village in ancient Egypt. After we finished with Egypt, we moved on to Greece and read the Iliad and Women of Troy.

In January, Mass Academy held its annual Day of Diversity. Every student gave a presentation about his or her family's cultural background and brought in an authentic food dish from his or her culture. I spoke about my family's journey from Germany to Israel during World War II and how my father moved to America when I was young.

We read Shakespeare's Richard III, and William Wordsworth's poetry. We finished the year with early American writers, such as Edgar Allen Poe and Stephen Crane.
